General Protection Data Protection Law (LGPD)

The LGPD is Brazil's comprehensive data protection regulation aimed at safeguarding personal data.

Description

The General Protection Data Protection Law (LGPD), officially known as Law No. 13,709/2018, was enacted in Brazil and came into effect in September 2020. It establishes a legal framework for the processing of personal data, aligning Brazil's data protection standards more closely with the European Union's General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). The LGPD applies to any individual or organization that processes personal data in Brazil, regardless of where the data processor is located. It sets forth principles for data processing, including transparency, purpose limitation, and data minimization. Importantly, the law grants individuals rights over their personal data, including the right to access, correct, delete, and port their data. Organizations must implement appropriate security measures to protect personal data and appoint a Data Protection Officer (DPO) to oversee compliance. The LGPD aims to enhance the protection of personal data in the digital age, ensuring that individuals have greater control over their information and establishing penalties for non-compliance.

Examples

  • A company must obtain explicit consent from users before collecting their personal data.
  • Individuals can request the deletion of their personal data from an organization's database.

Additional Information

  • The LGPD applies to both public and private sectors in Brazil.
  • Violations of the LGPD can result in fines of up to 2% of a company's revenue, capped at R$50 million.
